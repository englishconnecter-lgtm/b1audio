Opinions, Agreeing & Disagreeing

A B1 English lesson for expressing nuanced opinions, giving reasons, partly agreeing, politely disagreeing and managing discussion.

B1 English

Take part in a real discussion

Students build longer answers, support opinions with reasons and examples, and respond to different points of view politely.

LevelB1
Time75–90 minutes
FocusDiscussion skills
Start

Lesson overview

Students practise giving clear opinions, supporting them with examples and responding politely when they agree or disagree.

Lesson aims

  • Give opinions using a wider range of phrases.
  • Support opinions with reasons and examples.
  • Agree, partly agree and disagree politely.
  • Use linking words such as because, although, however and for example.
  • Participate in a structured group discussion.

Warm-up questions

  • Should students use phones in class?
  • Is working from home better than working in an office?
  • Should companies have a four-day work week?

Key idea

At B1 level, a good opinion is not just “I agree” or “I disagree”. It includes a reason, an example and a polite response to the other person’s view.

Dialogue

Discussing remote work

Read and practise the dialogue.

Emma: Personally, I think employees should be allowed to work from home three days a week.

Ravi: I see what you mean, but I’m not sure I completely agree. Some teams communicate better in the office.

Emma: That’s a fair point. However, working from home can save time and help people focus.

Ravi: I partly agree. It can be useful for individual tasks, although it may be harder for new employees to learn from the team.

Emma: That’s true. Maybe the best solution is a flexible system: office days for meetings and home days for focused work.

Ravi: I’d support that. It’s more balanced than working only from home or only from the office.

Grammar

Linking ideas in opinions

Use linking words to make your opinion clearer and more balanced.

because gives a reason: I agree because it saves money.

although shows contrast: I agree, although it may be difficult.

however introduces a different point: It is convenient. However, it can be expensive.

for example gives an example: Technology can help students. For example, they can use online dictionaries.

Speaking

Structured discussion cards

Choose a card. Discuss the topic in pairs or small groups. Use reasons, examples and polite responses.

Card A: Phones in class

Students should be allowed to use phones in class for learning activities.

Challenge: Have you considered distraction?

Card B: Remote work

Employees should work from home at least three days a week.

Challenge: Have you considered teamwork and training?

Card C: Four-day week

Companies should introduce a four-day work week with the same salary.

Challenge: Have you considered productivity and customer service?

Card D: Online learning

Online lessons can be as effective as classroom lessons.

Challenge: Have you considered motivation?

Card E: Public transport

Public transport should be free for students and young workers.

Challenge: Have you considered who pays for it?

Card F: Team projects

Team projects are better preparation for real work than individual assignments.

Challenge: Have you considered unequal participation?

Discussion checklist

  • Give one clear opinion.
  • Support it with one reason and one example.
  • Respond to another person’s point.
  • Partly agree or disagree politely.
  • Use at least two linking words.
  • Summarise what the group agrees on.
