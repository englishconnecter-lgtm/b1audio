Negotiations

A B1 Business English lesson for making offers, counteroffers, compromises and clear agreements.

B1 Business English

Make a deal that works

Students learn practical language for negotiating, meeting halfway and reaching agreement in business and everyday situations.

LevelB1
Time60–75 minutes
FocusNegotiating
Start

Lesson overview

Students practise negotiating in a business context and in a travel-planning context.

Lesson aims

  • Understand key negotiation vocabulary.
  • Listen for prices, quantities, preferences and compromise.
  • Use phrases for suggestions, preferences, counteroffers and agreement.
  • Take part in a short negotiation role-play.

Warm-up questions

  • When was the last time you had to compromise?
  • What makes a deal fair?
  • Is it easier to negotiate about money or plans?

Key idea

A good negotiation is not about winning everything. It is about finding an agreement that both sides can accept.

Vocabulary Pre-teaching

Key negotiation vocabulary

Review these words before listening.

compromise

an agreement where both sides give up something

deal

an agreement or arrangement

offer

to suggest something

counteroffer

a different suggestion in response to an offer

meet halfway

to compromise equally

fair

reasonable and equal for everyone

budget

the amount of money available to spend

flexible

willing to change or adapt

Activity 3

Useful phrases for negotiating

Review these key phrases used in negotiations.

Making Suggestions

  • What if we...?
  • How about...?
  • I was thinking we could...
  • Could we...?

Expressing Preferences

  • I'd prefer...
  • I'd rather...
  • I'm not really in the mood for...

Compromising

  • Could you meet us halfway?
  • That sounds fair.
  • Let's compromise.
  • What if we meet in the middle?

Making Counteroffers

  • However, if you...
  • What if we... instead?
  • I could offer... if you...

Reaching Agreement

  • That works for me.
  • Let's make it a deal.
  • That sounds like a good plan.
  • Perfect!

Speaking

Negotiation role-plays

Work in pairs. Try to reach an agreement.

Role-play 1: Supplier

You sell office chairs. Your normal price is €80 each. You can offer a discount if the customer orders more than 20 chairs.

Role-play 1: Customer

You need 15 office chairs. Your budget is €1,000. Negotiate the price, delivery date and payment terms.

Role-play 2: Trip planner A

You want a relaxing beach weekend. You do not want to spend too much money.

Role-play 2: Trip planner B

You prefer an active weekend with hiking or sightseeing. Try to find a compromise.

Review

Negotiation checklist

Use this checklist before your final speaking task.

  • Start with a clear offer or suggestion.
  • Explain your preference or limitation.
  • Make a counteroffer politely.
  • Try to meet halfway.
  • Confirm the final agreement clearly.
