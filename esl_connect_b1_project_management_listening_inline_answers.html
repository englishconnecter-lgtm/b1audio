Reading

Read the text about project management stages and discuss the questions with a partner.

The Five Stages of Project Management

Every successful project goes through five important stages. First is the initiation stage, where the team identifies the project goals and decides if the project is possible. Second is the planning stage, where the team creates a detailed plan, sets deadlines, and assigns tasks to team members.

The third stage is execution, when the team actually does the work. This is usually the longest stage. During execution, team members must collaborate effectively and communicate regularly. The fourth stage is monitoring, which happens at the same time as execution. The team leader checks progress, solves problems, and makes sure everyone is meeting their deadlines.

Finally, there is the closure stage. The team completes all final deliverables, presents the results, and evaluates what went well and what could be improved for future projects.

Discussion Questions

  1. Which stage do you think is the most important? Why?
  2. Have you ever worked on a group project? Which stage was the most difficult?
  3. Why is communication important during the execution stage?
  4. What can happen if a team doesn’t monitor progress regularly?

Dialogue

A project update meeting

Read and practise the dialogue.

Manager: Let’s start with a quick project update. How is the website launch going?

Ana: Overall, we’re making good progress. The design is finished, and the content is almost ready.

Manager: Good. Are there any risks at the moment?

Ana: Yes. The testing stage is behind schedule because we found a technical problem.

Manager: How serious is it?

Ana: It’s not critical, but we may need two extra days to fix it.

Manager: Okay. What are the next steps?

Ana: Marco will fix the issue by Wednesday, and I’ll send an update on Thursday morning.

Manager: Great. Let’s review the timeline again on Thursday.

Grammar

Present perfect and past simple in project updates

Use the present perfect for completed progress and the past simple for specific past actions.

Present perfect: We have finished the design. / The client has approved the plan.

Past simple: We finished the design yesterday. / The client approved the plan on Monday.

Project update pattern: We have completed X, but we still need to do Y.

Functional Language

Project update phrases

Practise more detailed language for giving updates, explaining timelines, raising risks and agreeing next steps in a project meeting.

1. Giving a clear project update

Use this when...Useful phrase
You want to give the general statusOverall, we’re making steady progress.
You completed a taskWe’ve completed...
You are working on a task nowWe’re currently working on...
You want to say the project is on timeEverything is on schedule.
You want to say there is a delayWe’re slightly behind schedule because...

2. Talking about timelines and deadlines

FunctionUseful phrase
Estimate a finish dateI estimate it will be finished by...
Give a personal deadlineI think I can complete it by...
State the final deadlineWe need it by... at the latest.
Explain available timeThat gives us...

3. Discussing problems and risks politely

FunctionUseful phrase
Introduce a concernI have a small concern about...
Ask about risksDoes anyone see any risks or challenges?
Explain a possible problemWe might have a problem with...
Talk about technical problemsIf we have any technical issues, we should...

4. Planning next steps and offering help

FunctionUseful phrase
Organise workLet me break down the tasks.
Assign helpI’ll assign someone to help you.
Ask if someone can do a taskCan you handle that?
Offer supportIf you need support, let me know.
Ask for updatesPlease keep everyone updated.

Speaking

Project meeting role cards

Work in groups. Hold a short project update meeting.

Card A: Project Manager

You lead the meeting. Ask about progress, risks and next steps.

Useful question: Are there any risks at the moment?

Card B: Designer

The design is finished, but the client asked for one small change.

Useful phrase: We have completed the design, but...

Card C: Content Writer

The text is in progress. You need two more days.

Useful phrase: The content is still in progress because...

Card D: Developer

Testing is behind schedule because there is a technical problem.

Useful phrase: We’re behind schedule because...

Meeting checklist

  • Say what has been completed.
  • Say what is still in progress.
  • Explain one risk or delay.
  • Agree at least two next steps.
  • Confirm who is responsible for each action.
